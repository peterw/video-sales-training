Video Cold Outreach - Sales Playbook

Goal: Close 3 deals at $2,500 each = $7,500 revenue

This playbook teaches the exact methodology for selling personalized video cold outreach services. We create AI-powered videos that make every cold email feel like it was made just for them. The video has their company name, their logo, their brand colors. It shows we did the work. It gets responses.

The Offer

What We Sell

Done-for-you personalized marketing videos. Each video is:

  • 60-90 seconds long
  • Branded with their company logo and colors
  • Professional motion graphics (Linear/Stripe aesthetic)
  • Delivered in 48-72 hours

Pricing

$2,500 flat fee per video

No retainers. No monthly fees. No upsells. One video, one price.

Why this price works: Video agencies charge $10,000-$20,000. We're 80% cheaper because we've automated production with AI. The prospect gets agency-quality at freelancer prices.

Deliverables

  1. Full 60-90 second professional video (MP4, 1080p)
  2. 3 aspect ratio versions (16:9, 9:16, 1:1)
  3. Source files upon request
  4. 1 round of revisions included

Ideal Customer Profile

Who They Are

Primary Target: B2B SaaS companies doing $1M-$20M ARR

Business Characteristics:

  • Annual revenue: $1M-$20M
  • Team size: 10-100 employees
  • Has a marketing team (even if just 1-2 people)
  • Currently doing outbound (cold email, LinkedIn, ads)
  • Product is visual or benefits from demonstration

Decision Maker Profile:

  • Title: Founder, CEO, Head of Marketing, VP Growth
  • Pain: Outbound response rates are declining
  • Sophistication: Understands content marketing, knows video works
  • Budget: Has discretionary spend for marketing experiments

Why They Buy

  1. Email is dying. Response rates have dropped 50% in the past 2 years. They need something that cuts through.
  2. Video works but they can't scale it. They know video gets 3x response rates. But making personalized videos manually takes hours per prospect.
  3. They've already tried everything else. Templates, AI personalization, LinkedIn automation. All commoditized. Video is the next frontier.
  4. Low risk, high reward. $2,500 to test. If it works, they scale. If it doesn't, they learned something.

Red Flags - Disqualify These Prospects

  • No outbound motion. If they're not already doing cold outreach, they won't use our videos.
  • Revenue under $500K. Can't afford $2,500 for an experiment.
  • Committee decisions. If they need 5 people to approve $2,500, the sales cycle is too long.
  • Agency-phobic. Some founders want to do everything in-house. Don't fight it.
  • No marketing person. Who will actually use the video once we deliver it?

Full Call Structure (10 Minutes)

This is a short call. They already saw the sample video. They already responded. Your job is to confirm fit, answer questions, and close.

Phase 1: Opening (30 seconds)

Goal: Get them talking about the video immediately.

"Hey [Name], thanks for hopping on. You saw the video I made for [Company] - what'd you think?"

Why this works:

  • They already saw your work. No need for rapport building.
  • Asking "what'd you think?" puts them in evaluation mode, not defense mode.
  • Their answer tells you how hot the lead is.

Listen for:

  • "It was really impressive" = Hot lead, move fast
  • "It was interesting" = Warm lead, needs more discovery
  • "I'm just exploring options" = Cold lead, qualify harder

Phase 2: Discovery (5 minutes)

Goal: Understand their current situation and pain.

Question 1: "What made you respond to the email?"

This reveals their buying trigger. Listen for:

  • "Our response rates are down" = Pain-driven
  • "We've been meaning to try video" = Aspiration-driven
  • "My co-founder forwarded it" = Lower intent, qualify harder

Question 2: "How are you currently creating video content?"

This reveals competition and budget:

  • "We don't" = Greenfield opportunity
  • "In-house team" = Position as supplement, not replacement
  • "Agency at $10K/video" = Easy price comparison win
  • "Loom/DIY" = Position as professional upgrade

Question 3: "What would having professional videos do for your marketing?"

This gets them to articulate the value in their own words. Whatever they say, repeat it back later when presenting.

Question 4: "What's your biggest marketing challenge right now?"

This broadens the conversation. Sometimes video isn't their real problem. If video doesn't solve their #1 challenge, the deal won't close.

Phase 3: Present (2 minutes)

Goal: Present the offer simply. No slides. No demos. They already saw the demo.

"Based on what you've told me, here's what I'd recommend: We create a full 60-90 second video for [Company]. - Your logo, your colors, your messaging - Same quality as what I sent you - Delivered in 48-72 hours - $2,500 flat fee One video. No retainer. No monthly commitment. If it works for your outreach, we can talk about doing more. But let's prove the concept first. What questions do you have?"

Why this works:

  • Simple. No tiers, no packages, no confusion.
  • "Prove the concept first" reduces perceived risk.
  • Ending with "what questions do you have?" invites objections you can handle.

Phase 4: Close (2 minutes)

Goal: Get commitment or clear next step.

"If you want to move forward, I can start today. We need: 1. Brand guidelines - logo, colors, fonts 2. Key messages you want in the video 3. 50% deposit ($1,250) I'll have the first draft to you in 48 hours. Does that work?"

If they say yes:

"Great. I'll send over an invoice right after this call. Once I see the payment come through, I'll send you a brief form to collect brand assets. Sound good?"

If they hesitate: Move to objection handling below.

Objection Handling

"$2,500 is too expensive"

"I get it. What do you think a video agency charges for this quality? [Wait for answer] Usually $10,000-$20,000. We're 80% less because we've automated production with AI. Think about it this way: if this video helps you close just one deal, what's that worth? If your average deal is $10K, you're at 4x ROI from a single closed deal. Is budget the real concern, or is there something else?"

"I need to think about it"

"Totally fair. What specifically are you weighing? [Wait for their answer - this reveals the real objection] [Address their specific concern] Is there anything else holding you back?"

Never accept "I need to think about it" at face value. There's always a specific concern underneath. Your job is to find it.

"I need to check with my team/co-founder"

"Makes sense. What questions do you think they'll have? [Write down their answers] Let's make sure you have all the info you need for that conversation. [Address each concern] When are you meeting with them? Can we schedule a follow-up call right after?"

Key insight: If they need team approval for $2,500, either they're not the decision maker or there's a deeper concern. Dig to find out which.

"Not the right time"

"Help me understand - what would make it the right time? [Listen carefully] [Option A: If timing is arbitrary] "What changes between now and then? The video quality stays the same, but right now you're losing deals to competitors who are using video. Every week you wait is another week of lower response rates." [Option B: If timing is real - budget cycle, launch, etc.] "That makes sense. Let's put time on the calendar for [their timeline]. In the meantime, would you want me to mock up what the video could look like so you're ready to move fast when the time is right?"

"We can do this in-house"

"You probably can. A few questions: 1. Who on your team would create it? 2. How long would it take them? 3. What's their hourly rate? [Wait for answers] So if [Person] spends 20 hours at $100/hour, that's $2,000 in labor cost, plus their time away from [their actual job]. And you don't have the video for 2-3 weeks instead of 48 hours. The question isn't whether you CAN do it in-house. It's whether you SHOULD."

  • Rotate across 5-10 domains to protect deliverability

Video Structure (20 seconds)

Every video follows the same 4-scene structure:

Scene 1: Hook (0-5 seconds)

Goal: Stop the scroll. Create curiosity.

Visual: Company logo animates in with brand colors Text: "Hey [Company], we made this for you" Audio: Upbeat background music starts

Scene 2: Problem (5-10 seconds)

Goal: Agitate the pain they already feel.

Visual: Clean typography with problem statement Text: "Your emails are getting ignored" "Response rates keep dropping" "Generic outreach doesn't work anymore"

Scene 3: Solution (10-15 seconds)

Goal: Position our video as the answer.

Visual: Product showcase / mockup Text: "Personalized video changes everything" "3x higher response rates" "Stand out in every inbox"

Scene 4: CTA (15-20 seconds)

Goal: Drive immediate action.

Visual: Logo + contact info Text: "Want the full version?" "Reply to this email" "[company website]"
